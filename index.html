Great Wall

One of the world's most famous landmarks, built to protect ancient China and now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Forbidden City

A former imperial palace that served Chinese emperors for over 500 years and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Summer Palace

A stunning imperial garden featuring beautiful lakes, traditional architecture, and peaceful landscapes.

The Temple of Heaven

Chinese emperors used it to pray here for good harvests. It is famous for its beautiful architecture and cultural importance.

The Palace Museum

The Palace Museum is a historic Beijing museum, once an imperial palace, displaying ancient Chinese artworks and treasures.

Jingshan Park

Jingshan Park is a historic park in Beijing known for its beautiful views of the Forbidden City and the city skyline.

Beihai Park

Beihai Park is one of Beijing's oldest imperial gardens, famous for its peaceful lake, White Dagoba, and beautiful scenery.

Gubei Water Town

Gubei Water Town is a charming town featuring traditional architecture, canals, and beautiful night views near the Great Wall.

ABOUT BEIJING

The Heart of China

Beijing is the capital of China and one of the world's oldest cities, with a history spanning over 3,000 years. Renowned for its rich cultural heritage, it is home to iconic landmarks such as the Great Wall, the Forbidden City, and the Temple of Heaven. Alongside its ancient history, Beijing is also a modern metropolis with impressive architecture, vibrant shopping districts, beautiful parks, and world-famous cuisine. Whether you're exploring historic palaces, tasting authentic Peking Duck, or experiencing the city's dynamic atmosphere, Beijing offers a unique journey where tradition and innovation come together.

FOODS IN BEIJING

Must-Try Local Dishes

Explore Beijing's most iconic dishes and uncover the stories behind every bite.

Peking Duck

Beijing's most iconic dish, Peking Duck is famous for its crispy golden skin and tender roasted meat. It is traditionally served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, cucumber, and scallions, creating a rich and flavorful dining experience.

Baozi

Baozi are soft, fluffy steamed buns filled with a variety of ingredients such as pork, vegetables, or sweet bean paste. They are commonly enjoyed as breakfast or a light meal throughout Beijing.

Beijing's Hotpot

Beijing Hot Pot is a communal dining experience where diners cook thin slices of meat, seafood, vegetables, and noodles in a simmering pot of flavorful broth. It's a warm and social meal often shared with family and friends.

Jianbing

Jianbing is one of Beijing's most popular street breakfasts. This crispy pancake is freshly cooked with egg, herbs, savory sauces, and a crunchy cracker, making it a quick and delicious meal on the go.

Tanghulu

Tanghulu is a traditional Chinese snack made by coating fresh fruit, usually hawthorn berries or strawberries, in a crisp layer of caramelized sugar. Its sweet and tangy flavor makes it a favorite treat during the colder months.

Zhajiangmian

Zhajiangmian is a beloved Beijing noodle dish made with thick wheat noodles, savory soybean paste, minced pork, and fresh vegetables.

Lüdagun

Lüdagun is a classic Beijing dessert made from glutinous rice rolls filled with sweet red bean paste and coated in fragrant roasted soybean flour. Its soft texture and nutty flavor have made it a local favorite for centuries.

Aiwowo

Aiwowo is a traditional Beijing dessert made from glutinous rice dough filled with sesame, walnuts, peanuts, and dried fruit. Once served in the imperial palace, it remains a popular sweet treat today.

Douzhi

Douzhi is a unique fermented mung bean drink that has been enjoyed in Beijing for hundreds of years. Its distinctive sour taste may surprise first-time visitors, but it remains an important part of the city's culinary tradition.
